Communication & Tone Guidelines

A reference tool for learning design, mapping how communication styles impact the anxious learner. Written from the perspective of the learner.

Supportive Tone
Stress-Inducing Tone
Tell me exactly what you expect from me.
Leave the real expectations vague or implied.
Use neutral, plain language that doesn't judge my ability.
Use authoritative language that makes me feel evaluated.
Break complex tasks nicely into step-by-step instructions.
Hand me dense, overwhelming paragraphs of text.
Acknowledge that feeling confused is a normal part of learning.
Speak as if I should already know the material.
Keep the layout predictable and use consistent phrasing.
Change the structure or phrasing unexpectedly.
Focus your feedback on my progress and what I understand.
Focus mainly on my errors without offering a path forward.
Explain the context before giving me an urgent deadline.
Create a sense of urgency without explaining why it matters.
